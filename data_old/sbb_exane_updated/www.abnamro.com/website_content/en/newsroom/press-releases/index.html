ABN AMRO Digital Impact Fund doubles to EUR 100m

ABN AMRO's Digital Impact Fund (DIF) has doubled its assets under management from EUR 50 million to EUR 100 million and will continue its activities under the name ABN AMRO Ventures. ABN AMRO increases its equity exposure

ABN AMRO has decided to increase its exposure to stocks at the expense of cash, and has now adopted a neutral stance on the asset class. Bonds remain out of favour. In terms of regions, US stocks continue to be overweight (relative to the benchmark) and European stocks underweight, although both have been reduced somewhat. A number of changes have also been made at sector level, where ABN AMRO prefers the healthcare and information technology sectors.

ABN AMRO nominates Laetitia Griffith as a member of the Supervisory Board

ABN AMRO has announced today that its Supervisory Board will nominate Laetitia Griffith for appointment to the Supervisory Board of ABN AMRO Bank N.V. for a four-year term of office. The proposal to appoint Laetitia Griffith will be put forward at the Extraordinary General Meeting (EGM) on 17 December 2019. The appointment of Laetitia Griffith will be subject to the approval of the European Central Bank.

Press release29 October 2019

ABN AMRO sees first shoots of economic improvement

Despite some small, early signs pointing to possible coming improvement in the global economy, ABN AMRO continues to prefer cash over stocks and bonds. ABN AMRO reckons risks and uncertainty still dominate, such as the ongoing US/China trade dispute and China’s economic slowdown. For that reason, equities and bonds are kept at underweight.

Sandra Phlippen new Chief Economist at ABN AMRO

Sandra Phlippen has been appointed ABN AMRO’s new Chief Economist with effect from 1 December. In her new role, Sandra will also replace Han de Jong as head of the Group Economics department.

Press release18 October 2019

Tikkie increasingly used for monthly outgoings

The Dutch don’t just use Tikkie to repay their share of gifts or an evening out: ABN AMRO’s payment service is increasingly also used by people to pay their bit of shared outgoings, varying from insurance to Netflix and Spotify subscriptions. Tikkies to repay shopping expenses are the biggie, though: in the year to the end of September, 30% more Tikkie messages were marked shopping than in 2018.

Press release17 October 2019

ABN AMRO’s Digital Impact Fund increases stake in CrossLend

ABN AMRO’s Digital Impact Fund (DIF) has increased its stake in CrossLend, the Berlin-based digital cross-border debt marketplace. Following its first investment in September 2018, ABN AMRO DIF again participated in the latest funding round led by Santander InnoVentures together with existing shareholders Lakestar and Earlybird.

Press release16 October 2019

Low interest rates give the housing market another boost

Home sales are surging, driven by the low mortgage interest rates. Where ABN AMRO’s analysts had previously predicted a five percent drop, they now expect home sales to stabilise as low mortgage costs make buying more affordable. At the same time, house prices continue to rise as available homes remain in short supply.

ABN AMRO, Rabobank and BNP Paribas team up with Neumann Kaffee Gruppe (NKG), IDH, and USAID to support smallholder coffee farmers in Kenya, Honduras and Mexico.

ABN AMRO subject of investigation by the Dutch public prosecutor

On Wednesday 25 September 2019, the Dutch public prosecutor informed ABN AMRO that the bank is the subject of an investigation relating to requirements under the Act on the prevention of money laundering and financing of terrorism (in Dutch: Wwft). ABN AMRO will cooperate fully with the investigation.
